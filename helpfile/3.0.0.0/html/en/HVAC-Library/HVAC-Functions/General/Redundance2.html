Redundance2 (FB)¶

FUNCTION_BLOCK Redundance2

Short Description¶

Redundant control of two devices similar in type
Influencing factors: requirements, operating hours, operating and fault states
Typical application: Definition of the lead and follower pump

Functional Description¶

General¶

This function block is used for the redundant control of two similar devices depending on the influencing variables requirements ( xEnOne and xEnTwo ), operating hours ( uiOperatingHours1 and uiOperatingHours2 ), operating states ( xFb1 and xFb2 ), alarm states ( xAlarm1 and xAlarm2 ) and the operating mode for the sequence definition ( eModeRedundance ).
The release of the master device and, if required, of the slave device is determined internally and transferred to the device outputs xCmd1 or xCmd2 taking into account the current sequence iCurrentSequence.

xEnOne and xEnTwo requirements¶

They are used to define whether only the lead device or the lead and the follower device should be requested.

Fault output xErr¶

It indicates a discrepancy between the requirements and the fault states of the devices.
The constellations are described in the function table that activate the xErr output (= TRUE), that is, indicate a fault.
In all other cases, the xErr output is not active (= FALSE).

Output iCurrentSequence¶

It always displays the current sequence of the two devices.
The first digit always indicates the device number of the lead device (example: iCurrentSequence = 1X ... Lead device = Device 1)
The second digit always indicates the device number of the follower device. (Example: iCurrentSequence = X2 ... Follower = Device 2)

Setpoint / Parameter xConOperatingHours¶

In the state TRUE, each time the function block is processed, the sequential switching is triggered depending on the operating hours and operating messages ( with xEnFb = TRUE ) of devices 1 and 2.
In the state FALSE, the sequential switching can only be triggered by a rising edge at the xUpdate input.

Setpoint / Parameter xEnFb¶

In the state TRUE, the operating states of devices 1 and 2 are evaluated and taken into account in the sequential circuit.
In the state FALSE, the device outputs of Devices 1 and 2 are evaluated and taken into account in the sequential switching.

Setpoint / Parameter wTolerance¶

This setpoint determines the maximum operating hours difference before a switching over of the lead and follower device on the basis of the operating hours.

Setpoint / parameter eModeRedundance¶

This setpoint determines the operating mode of the sequence definition. In automatic mode, the sequence is determined automatically.
The forced definition of the sequence in the ForceComponent1 or ForceComponent2 operating modes is carried out by selectively influencing the internal statuses of the operating hours counters or the setpoint / parameter wTolerance.
Sequence switching in the event of a fault is therefore still possible.

Device outputs xCmd1 and xCmd2¶

The device outputs are finally defined as follows:

Lead device = Device 1

xCmd1 = TRUE: The lead device is requested and at least one device is requested and device 1 is not disturbed

xCmd2 = TRUE: The lead device is requested and at least one device is requested and device 2 is not disturbed

Lead device = Device 2

xCmd1 = TRUE: The follower device is requested and at least one device is requested and device 1 is not disturbed

xCmd1 = TRUE: The lead device is requested and at least one device is requested and device 2 is not disturbed
